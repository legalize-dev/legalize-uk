# Universal Credit (Removal of Two Child Limit) Act 2026

#### Removal of two child limit: GB

##### 1

- (1) In [section 10](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2012/5/section/10) of the [Welfare Reform Act 2012](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2012/5/contents) (responsibility for children and young persons)—

- (a) omit [subsection (1A)](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2012/5/section/10) (the two child limit), and

- (b) in [subsection (4)](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2012/5/section/10) omit “or (1A)”.

- (2) In consequence of the amendments made by [subsection (1)](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2026/13/section/1/1/enacted)—

- (a) in [section 14](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2016/7/section/14) of the [Welfare Reform and Work Act 2016](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2016/7/contents), omit [subsections (2)](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2016/7/section/14), [(4)](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2016/7/section/14) and [(5)](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2016/7/section/14)[(a)](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2016/7/section/14), and

- (b) in [regulation 24](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/uksi/2013/376/regulation/24)[(1)](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/uksi/2013/376/regulation/24) of the [Universal Credit Regulations 2013 (S.I. 2013/376)](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/uksi/2013/376/contents), omit “and in respect of whom an amount may be included under section 10”.

- (3) The following provisions of the [Universal Credit Regulations 2013](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/uksi/2013/376/contents) were made under the power repealed by [subsection (1)](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2026/13/section/1/1/enacted)[(b)](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2026/13/section/1/1/b/enacted) (power to provide for exceptions to the two child limit) and are therefore revoked—

- (a) [regulation 24A](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/uksi/2013/376/regulation/24A);

- (b) [regulation 24B](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/uksi/2013/376/regulation/24B);

- (c) [Schedule 12](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/uksi/2013/376/schedule/12).

- (4) The amendments made by [this section](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2026/13/section/1/enacted) have effect in relation to assessment periods commencing on or after 6 April 2026, and for this purpose “*assessment period*” has the same meaning as in [Part 1](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2012/5/part/1) of the [Welfare Reform Act 2012](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2012/5/contents).

#### Removal of two child limit: NI

##### 2

- (1) In [Article 15](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/nisi/2015/2006/article/15) of the [Welfare Reform (Northern Ireland) Order 2015 (S.I. 2015/2006 (N.I. 1))](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/nisi/2015/2006/contents) (responsibility for children and young persons)—

- (a) omit [paragraph (1A)](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/nisi/2015/2006/article/15) (the two child limit), and

- (b) in [paragraph (4)](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/nisi/2015/2006/article/15), omit “or (1A)”.

- (2) In consequence of the amendments made by [subsection (1)](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2026/13/section/2/1/enacted)—

- (a) in [Article 10](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/nisi/2016/999/article/10) of the [Welfare Reform and Work (Northern Ireland) Order 2016 (S.I. 2016/999 (N.I. 1))](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/nisi/2016/999/contents), omit paragraphs (2), (4) and (5)(a), and

- (b) in regulation 25(1) of the [Universal Credit Regulations (Northern Ireland) 2016 (S.R. (N.I.) 2016 No. 216)](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/nisr/2016/216/contents), omit “and in respect of whom an amount may be included under Article 15”.

- (3) The following provisions of the [Universal Credit Regulations (Northern Ireland) 2016](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/nisr/2016/216/contents) were made under the power repealed by [subsection (1)](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2026/13/section/2/1/enacted)[(b)](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2026/13/section/2/1/b/enacted) (power to provide for exceptions to the two child limit) and are therefore revoked—

- (a) [regulation 25A](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/nisr/2016/216/regulation/25A);

- (b) [regulation 25B](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/nisr/2016/216/regulation/25B);

- (c) [Schedule 12](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/nisr/2016/216/schedule/12).

- (4) The amendments made by [this section](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2026/13/section/2/enacted) have effect in relation to assessment periods commencing on or after 6 April 2026, and for this purpose “*assessment period*” has the same meaning as in [Part 2](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/nisi/2015/2006/part/2) of the [Welfare Reform (Northern Ireland) Order 2015](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/nisi/2015/2006/contents).

#### Extent, commencement and short title

##### 3

- (1) [Section 1](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2026/13/section/1/enacted) extends to England and Wales and Scotland only.

- (2) [Section 2](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2026/13/section/2/enacted) extends to Northern Ireland only.

- (3) [This section](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2026/13/section/3/enacted) extends to England and Wales, Scotland and Northern Ireland.

- (4) [Sections 1](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2026/13/section/1/enacted) and [2](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2026/13/section/2/enacted) come into force on 6 April 2026.

- (5) [This section](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2026/13/section/3/enacted) comes into force on the day on which this Act is passed.

- (6) The Secretary of State may by regulations made by statutory instrument make transitional or saving provision in connection with the coming into force of [section 1](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2026/13/section/1/enacted).

- (7) The Department for Communities in Northern Ireland may by regulations made by statutory rule for the purposes of the [Statutory Rules (Northern Ireland) Order 1979 (S.I. 1979/1573 (N.I. 12))](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/nisi/1979/1573/contents) make transitional or saving provision in connection with the coming into force of [section 2](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2026/13/section/2/enacted).

- (8) This Act may be cited as the Universal Credit (Removal of Two Child Limit) Act 2026.
