# House of Lords (Hereditary Peers) Act 2026

#### Exclusion of remaining hereditary peers

##### 1

Omit [section 2](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1999/34/section/2) of the [House of Lords Act 1999](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1999/34/contents) (exception to exclusion of hereditary peers from membership of House of Lords).

#### Resignation

##### 2

In [section 1](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2014/24/section/1) of the [House of Lords Reform Act 2014](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2014/24/contents) (resignation), after [subsection (4)](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2014/24/section/1) insert—

> (5) Where a notice under this section is given and signed by a person on behalf of a peer who lacks capacity to give or sign the notice, the notice must be given and signed in accordance with Standing Orders of the House.

#### Claims to hereditary peerages

##### 3

- (1) The jurisdiction of the House of Lords in relation to claims to hereditary peerages is abolished.

- (2) For the purposes of [this section](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2026/12/section/3/enacted) a claim to a hereditary peerage includes a claim to a hereditary peerage in abeyance.

#### Consequential amendments

##### 4

- (1) In the [Peerage Act 1963](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1963/48/contents)—

- (a) in [section 1](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1963/48/section/1)[(2)](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1963/48/section/1) (disclaimer of certain hereditary peerages), omit the words from “; and no such instrument” to the end;

- (b) omit section 4 (Scottish peerages) and the italic heading before it;

- (c) omit section 6 (peeresses in own right).

- (2) In the [House of Lords Act 1999](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1999/34/contents)—

- (a) omit section 3(2) (disqualifications in relation to House of Commons applicable to hereditary peers);

- (b) omit paragraph 1 of Schedule 1 (amendment of Peerage Act 1963).

- (3) In the [Constitutional Reform and Governance Act 2010](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2010/25/contents), in [section 42](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2010/25/section/42) (tax status of members of House of Lords: transitional provision)—

- (a) omit subsections (3) and (4);

- (b) in [subsection (5)](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2010/25/section/42), omit “If M is not such a person,”;

- (c) in [subsection (8)](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2010/25/section/42)—

- (i) omit “or M succeeds to a peerage”;

- (ii) omit the words from “If subsection (3)(a)” to the end;

- (d) omit subsection (9).

- (4) In the [House of Lords Reform Act 2014](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2014/24/contents), in [section 4](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2014/24/section/4) (effect of ceasing to be a member)—

- (a) in [subsection (3)](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2014/24/section/4), omit “, by virtue of a hereditary peerage”;

- (b) omit subsection (4);

- (c) in [subsection (5)](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2014/24/section/4), omit “other than a hereditary peer”;

- (d) omit subsection (7).

#### Extent and commencement

##### 5

- (1) An amendment or repeal made by [section 1](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2026/12/section/1/enacted), [2](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2026/12/section/2/enacted) or [4](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2026/12/section/4/enacted) has the same extent as the provision amended or repealed.

- (2) [Section 3](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2026/12/section/3/enacted), [this section](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2026/12/section/5/enacted) and [section 6](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2026/12/section/6/enacted) extend to England and Wales, Scotland and Northern Ireland.

- (3) Section [2](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2026/12/section/2/enacted), this section and [section 6](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2026/12/section/6/enacted) come into force on the day on which this Act is passed.

- (4) The other provisions of this Act come into force at the end of the Session of Parliament in which this Act is passed.

- (5) Accordingly, any writ of summons issued for the present Parliament in right of a hereditary peerage is of no effect after that Session.

#### Short title

##### 6

This Act may be cited as the House of Lords (Hereditary Peers) Act 2026.
