# Worker Protection (Amendment of Equality Act 2010) Act 2023

#### Employer duty to prevent sexual harassment of employees

##### 1

After section 40 of the Equality Act 2010 insert—

> (40A)
>   (1) An employer (A) must take reasonable steps to prevent sexual harassment of employees of A in the course of their employment.
>   (2) “Sexual harassment” in [subsection (1)](#p00125) means harassment of the kind described in section 26(2) (unwanted conduct of a sexual nature).
>   (3) A contravention of [subsection (1)](#p00125) (or a contravention of section 111 or 112 that relates to a contravention of [subsection (1)](#p00125)) is enforceable as an unlawful act under Part 1 of the Equality Act 2006 (and, by virtue of section 120(8) and (9), is enforceable only by the Commission under that Part or by an employment tribunal in accordance with [section 124A](#p00175) (compensation uplift in employee sexual harassment cases)).

#### Enforcement of duty to prevent sexual harassment of employees

##### 2

In section 120 of the Equality Act 2010 (jurisdiction of employment tribunal)—

- (a) in subsection (8), for “a reference to section” substitute “references to section [40A](#p00123)[(1)](#p00125) or”;

- (b) after that subsection insert—

> (9) Subsection (8) does not prevent an employment tribunal from considering a contravention of [section 40A](#p00123)[(1)](#p00125) in accordance with [section 124A](#p00175) (compensation uplift in employee sexual harassment cases).

#### Sexual harassment of employees: compensation uplift

##### 3

After section 124 of the Equality Act 2010 insert—

> (124A)
>   (1) This section applies where—
>     (a) an employment tribunal has found that there has been a contravention of section 40 (harassment of employees) which involved, to any extent, harassment of the kind described in section 26(2) (sexual harassment), and
>     (b) the tribunal has ordered the respondent to pay compensation to the complainant under section 124(2)(b).
>   (2) The tribunal must consider whether and to what extent the respondent has also contravened [section 40A](#p00123)[(1)](#p00125) (duty to take reasonable steps to prevent harassment of employees).
>   (3) If the tribunal is satisfied that the respondent has contravened [section 40A](#p00123)[(1)](#p00125), it may order the respondent to pay an amount to the complainant (a “compensation uplift”) in addition to the compensation amount determined in accordance with section 124(6).
>   (4) The amount of the compensation uplift—
>     (a) must reflect the extent to which, in the tribunal’s opinion, the respondent has contravened [section 40A](#p00123)[(1)](#p00125), but
>     (b) may be no more than 25% of the amount awarded under section 124(2)(b).

#### Consequential amendments to the Equality Act 2006

##### 4

- (1) Part 1 of the Equality Act 2006 (the Commission for Equality and Human Rights) is amended as follows.

- (2) In section 21 (unlawful act notice), after subsection (7) insert—

> (8) Subsection (7) applies as though a claim could be made to an employment tribunal in respect of—
>   (a) an alleged contravention of section [40A](#p00123)[(1)](#p00125) of the Equality Act 2010 (duty to take reasonable steps to prevent harassment of employees), or
>   (b) an alleged contravention of section 111 or 112 of that Act which relates to a contravention of section [40A](#p00123)[(1)](#p00125) of that Act.

- (3) In section 24A (enforcement powers: supplemental), in subsection (1), after paragraph (a) insert—

> (aa) an act which is unlawful because it amounts to a contravention of [section 40A](#p00123)[(1)](#p00125) of that Act (or to a contravention of section 111 or 112 of that Act that relates to a contravention of [section 40A](#p00123)[(1)](#p00125) of that Act) (employer duty to take reasonable steps to prevent sexual harassment of employees),

.

#### Extent, commencement and short title

##### 5

- (1) This Act extends to England and Wales and Scotland.

- (2) This section comes into force on the day on which this Act is passed.

- (3) Sections [1](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2023/51/section/1/2024-10-26) to [4](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2023/51/section/4/2024-10-26) come into force at the end of the period of one year beginning with the day on which this Act is passed.

- (4) This Act may be cited as the Worker Protection (Amendment of Equality Act 2010) Act 2023.
