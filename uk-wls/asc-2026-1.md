# Mental Health Review Tribunal for Wales (Membership) Act 2026

#### Membership of the Mental Health Review Tribunal for Wales

##### 1

- (1) Paragraph 1 of Schedule 2 to the [Mental Health Act 1983 (c. 20)](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1983/20) is amended as follows.

- (2) The existing provision becomes sub-paragraph (1).

- (3) After sub-paragraph (1) insert—

> (2) In sub-paragraph (1)(b), “*registered medical practitioner*” means a fully registered person within the meaning of the Medical Act 1983 (whether or not that person holds a licence to practise under that Act).
> (3) If, before the coming into force of sub-paragraph (2), a person appointed as a medical member under this paragraph did not hold a licence to practise under the Medical Act 1983 (whether at the time of appointment or at any other time), that fact does not affect the validity of—
>   (a) the person’s appointment to the Mental Health Review Tribunal for Wales, or
>   (b) the person’s membership of that tribunal during that period.

#### Training for non-practising medical members

##### 2

- (1) The Welsh Ministers must carry out a review of the training arrangements maintained by the President of Welsh Tribunals, in accordance with section 60(5)(a) of the [Wales Act 2017 (c. 4)](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2017/4), for medical members of the [Mental Health Review Tribunal for Wales who do not hold licences to practise under the Medical Act 1983 (c. 54)](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1983/54).

- (2) The Welsh Ministers must publish a report of the review no later than 12 months after the day on which this Act comes into force.

#### Coming into force

##### 3

This Act comes into force on the day after the day on which it receives Royal Assent.

#### Short title

##### 4

This Act may be referred to as—

- (a) the Mental Health Review Tribunal for Wales (Membership) Act 2026, or

- (b) Deddf Tribiwnlys Adolygu Iechyd Meddwl Cymru (Aelodaeth) 2026.
